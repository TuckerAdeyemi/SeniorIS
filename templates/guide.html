🌌 Galaxy Classifier

Classifier Galaxy Guide

Elliptical Galaxies

These galaxies are shaped like elongated spheres or "ellipsoids." They contain older stars and very little gas or dust. Standard (E0-E7): These follow a smooth, featureless light profile, ranging from perfectly circular to highly elongated. With Oddities: Some exhibit "peculiar" features, such as shells, ripples, or dual nuclei, often caused by the recent gravitational absorption of a smaller galaxy.

Spiral Galaxies

Characterized by a rotating disc with "arms" of stars and gas. Central Bulge: The classic spiral features a dense, spherical group of old stars at the center. Without Bulge (Pure-Disk): Also known as "late-type" spirals, these lack a prominent central cluster, consisting almost entirely of a thin disk. With Oddities: These include "flocculent" spirals with patchy, discontinuous arms or "barred" spirals where arms emerge from a straight bar of stars rather than the core.

Irregular Galaxies

These galaxies lack a distinct regular shape, like a spiral or an elliptical. They are often chaotic in appearance, rich in gas and dust, and are hotbeds for new star formation. They are frequently the result of galactic collisions or near-misses that rip apart more organized structures.
